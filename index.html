Final Fantasy

The game that has touched the hearts of millions of players worldwide!

A history of how the game has developed:

  • 1987 - Hironobu Sakaguchi develops Final Fantasy I (FFI), the first in the series, creating the company's flagship franchise. The game was a massive success which pulled the company out of bankruptcy. Had the game not been successful Square (previous name before Square Enix) would have gone out of business. The series so many have come to love around the world would cease to exist today!
  • 1988 - Final Fantasy II gets released in Japan for the family computer and is later re-released for PlayStation, game boy advance, and other platforms.
  • 1990 - Final Fantasy I gets released in North America and outperforms some of its major competitors. Final Fantasy III is released and is one of the most successful selling games in the series at 1.4 million copies sold in Japan. This is later re-released to Nintendo Ds in 2006.
  • 1991 - Final Fantasy IV is released for the Super NES. It has commonly been known to be called Final Fantasy II after being the second installment to be released outside of Japan. It sold 4 million copies worldwide.
  • 1992 - Final Fantasy V is released for Nintendo's Super Famicom. The job system feature was added allowing players to customize their characters. It sold 2 million copies worldwide.
  • 1994 - Final Fantasy VI is released. It was designed by Hiroyuki Ito who was also the director. It was based on a fictional second industrial revolution. This version has 14 playable characters.
  • 1997 - Final Fantasy VII is released for the PlayStation. It was designed by Hironobu Sakaguchi. Over 13 million copies were sold worldwide.
  • 1999 - Final Fantasy VIII is released. This is the first game to feature realistic characters, magic points, and the vocal piece as the theme music. Total sales reached $151 million.
  • 2000 - Final Fantasy IX is released for the PlayStation. This version sold over 5.5 million copies worldwide.
  • 2001 - Final Fantasy X is released for PlayStation 2 and is later re-released for PlayStation 3 in 2013. Over 8.5 million copies were sold worldwide.
  • 2002 - Final Fantasy XI is released for PlayStation 2 and Microsoft Windows. It is the first massively multiplayer online role-playing game that Square Enix developed in the final fantasy series.
  • 2006 - Final Fantasy XII is released for the PlayStation 2. It has an open world, a seamless battle system, and a gambit system that allows players to customize the artificial intelligence of their characters.
  • 2009 - Final Fantasy XIII is released for PlayStation 3 and XBOX 360. It is the first installment to feature fast-paced combat. It sold 1.7 million copies in Japan.
  • 2010 - Final Fantasy XIV started development in 2005 under code name "Rapture" and was released in 2010 for Microsoft Windows and PlayStation 3. It included a multiplayer mode enabling players to connect with others globally. There are a total of 35 million active players with over 3 million daily players.
  • 2016 - Final Fantasy XV is released for PlayStation 4 and Xbox one. It featured an open world map and new quick battle system. It also featured a vehicle travel system and camping. This only sold about 1 million copies in Japan.
  • 2023 - The latest and greatest addition to the FF family, Final Fantasy XVI, is set to be released in the summer of 2023 for the PlayStation 5!
